## Session Structure (60-Minute Template)

### Minutes 0-10: Review & Warm-Up

**Objective:** Assess progress since last session and set the tone.

- Review homework completion. Did the student solve the assigned puzzles? Did they play the recommended number of practice games?
- Quick review of 1-2 recent games the student played. Focus on whether they applied concepts from the previous lesson.
- Ask the student: "What went well this week? What frustrated you?"
- Identify the theme for today based on observed weaknesses.

**Coach preparation (before session):** Review any games the student submitted. Note 2-3 key moments to discuss. Have positions loaded and ready in the board tool.

### Minutes 10-40: Targeted Lesson

**Objective:** Teach one focused concept addressing the student's biggest current weakness.

- Present the concept with 2-3 example positions. Explain the idea clearly before asking the student to try.
- Use the Socratic method: ask "What would you play here?" before showing the answer. Give the student 30-60 seconds to think.
- Show the engine evaluation (Pikafish) to validate or challenge the student's intuition.
- Connect the lesson to the student's own games: "Remember in your Tuesday game, you had a similar position here..."
- Cover one concept thoroughly rather than multiple concepts superficially.

**Common lesson topics by level:**
- Beginner: Piece coordination, basic checkmate patterns, simple tactics (forks, pins)
- Intermediate: Opening repertoire building, middlegame planning, complex tactics, basic endgames
- Advanced: Deep opening preparation, positional judgment, complex endgame technique, tournament psychology

### Minutes 40-55: Practice & Guided Play

**Objective:** Immediately apply the lesson in realistic positions.

- Present 3-5 practice positions related to the lesson topic. Increase difficulty progressively.
- For each position, have the student think aloud. This reveals their thought process, which is more valuable than just the answer.
- If time permits, play out a position against the student from a relevant starting point. Deliberately create situations where the lesson applies.
- Provide immediate feedback: "Good instinct, but consider this alternative..." or "You found it — that is exactly the pattern we discussed."

### Minutes 55-60: Homework & Goal Setting

**Objective:** Give the student clear tasks for the week and reinforce motivation.

- Assign specific homework:
  - 5-10 puzzle positions related to today's topic (provide FEN strings or images)
  - 2-3 practice games on a specific platform with a specific opening to practice
  - One game to annotate themselves (write what they were thinking at each critical moment)
- Set one measurable goal for the week: "Win at least one game using the cannon sacrifice pattern" or "Reach 1400 on 天天象棋"
- Confirm next session date and time
- Ask if the student has any remaining questions

## Student Intake Form

Send this form to every new student before the first session. Use Google Forms, Tally, or a simple document.

### Questions

**Background**
1. What is your name and preferred language for coaching? (English / 中文 / bilingual)
2. How long have you been playing xiangqi?
3. How did you learn? (Self-taught, family, school, online, other)
4. Do you have experience with international chess or other board games?

**Current Level**
5. What is your current online rating? (Platform and rating, e.g., 天天象棋 1350, PlayOK 1500)
6. How many games do you play per week on average?
7. Do you study xiangqi outside of playing games? If so, how? (Books, videos, puzzles, engine analysis)
8. Rate your confidence in these areas (1-5):
   - Opening knowledge: ___
   - Tactical ability: ___
   - Endgame technique: ___
   - Time management: ___
   - Psychological composure: ___

**Goals**
9. What is your primary goal with coaching? (Improve rating, prepare for tournament, enjoy the game more, learn systematically, other)
10. What specific rating or achievement are you targeting in the next 6 months?
11. How much time per week can you dedicate to xiangqi study outside of sessions?

**Logistics**
12. What is your time zone?
13. What days/times generally work for you?
14. Do you have a preferred communication platform for between-session support? (WeChat / Discord / Email / Telegram)

**Games for Review**
15. Please submit 3 recent games (PGN files, screenshots, or game links from online platforms). Choose games that represent your typical play — ideally one win, one loss, and one game you found confusing.

## Tools & Technology Setup

### Essential Tools

**Board Tool for Screen Sharing**
- **Primary:** Use a web-based xiangqi board that both coach and student can see during screen share. Recommended options:
  - Vschess (web-based, free, supports FEN input)
  - 鹏飞象棋 PengFei (feature-rich board editor)
  - Custom board tool if the channel has one built
- The coach shares their screen showing the board. Pieces are moved in real-time as positions are discussed.

**Engine Analysis**
- **Pikafish** (open-source xiangqi engine, strongest available) running locally on the coach's machine
- Use engine evaluation to validate positions during lessons and provide objective assessments of student games
- Do NOT show engine lines during the "student thinking" phase — only after the student commits to a move

**Video Conferencing**
- **Zoom** — reliable, recording available, screen share works well
- **腾讯会议 (Tencent Meeting)** — preferred for Chinese-speaking students, integrated with WeChat
- **Discord** — good for students already in the community server, free, screen share built-in
- Record every session (with student permission) so the student can re-watch

**File Sharing**
- Use Google Drive or 百度网盘 for sharing PGN files, puzzle packs, and session notes
- Maintain a folder per student with all their materials organized by date

### Nice-to-Have Tools
- **Lichess study feature** — while designed for international chess, the study/chapter structure is a good model for organizing lesson plans
- **Notion database** — track student progress, lesson history, and homework completion
- **OBS Studio** — if recording sessions for the student's review library

## Scheduling System

### Recommended: Calendly (Free Tier)

**Setup Steps:**
1. Create a Calendly account with coaching-specific branding
2. Set event type: "Xiangqi Coaching Session — 60 min"
3. Configure available time slots. Account for multiple time zones (key markets: US, China, Southeast Asia)
4. Set buffer time: 15 minutes between sessions for notes and preparation
5. Enable automatic reminders: 24 hours and 1 hour before session
6. Add intake form link to the confirmation email for first-time bookings

**Scheduling Rules:**
- Maximum 4 sessions per day to maintain quality
- No back-to-back sessions (15 min buffer minimum)
- Block off preparation time: 15 min before each session for reviewing the student's games and loading positions
- Offer consistent weekly slots for monthly subscribers (e.g., "Every Tuesday 7pm")

**Alternative for Chinese market:** 小鹅通 (Xiaoe-tech) has built-in scheduling and payment for Chinese audiences.

### Payment Workflow

**Single Sessions:**
- Student books via Calendly
- Payment link sent automatically upon booking (Stripe payment link or PayPal.me)
- Session confirmed only after payment received
- 24-hour cancellation policy for full refund; under 24 hours, session is forfeited or rescheduled once

**Monthly Subscriptions:**
- Set up recurring billing via Stripe Subscriptions or PayPal Subscriptions
- Billing on the 1st of each month
- Student can cancel with 7 days notice before next billing cycle
- Unused sessions do not roll over (encourages consistent practice)

**Chinese payment flow:**
- For WeChat Pay / Alipay, use 小鹅通 or 知识星球 as an intermediary platform that handles payments and provides a professional storefront
- Alternatively, accept direct WeChat transfers for trusted long-term students (less professional but simpler)

## Scaling the Coaching Business

### Phase 1: Solo Coach (Months 1-6)
- Take on 5-10 students maximum
- Refine session structure and materials based on feedback
- Build a library of reusable lesson positions and puzzles
- Target revenue: $400-$1,200/month

### Phase 2: Documented System (Months 7-12)
- Document all lesson plans and create a coaching playbook
- Build a library of 200+ categorized positions for lessons
- Create pre-built homework packs for common student levels
- Introduce group sessions (3-4 students, $15/person/hour) for lower price point
- Target revenue: $1,000-$2,500/month

### Phase 3: Coach Network (Year 2+)
- Recruit 1-2 additional coaches (students who have completed Tier 3 and show teaching aptitude)
- Provide them with the coaching playbook and materials
- Take a 20-30% platform fee on their sessions
- Focus personal coaching on advanced/premium students only
- Target revenue: $2,000-$5,000/month

## Policies

### Cancellation & Rescheduling
- 24+ hours notice: Full reschedule or refund
- Under 24 hours: Session forfeited (monthly plans) or no refund (single sessions)
- Coach cancellation: Session rescheduled at student's convenience, or prorated refund
- No-show (student): Counted as completed session after 15-minute wait

### Satisfaction Guarantee
- First session: 100% money-back guarantee if the student is unsatisfied for any reason
- Monthly plans: Can cancel anytime with 7 days notice; no refund for current month
- This lowers the barrier to trying coaching and demonstrates confidence in the service
