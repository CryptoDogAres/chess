## Weekly Schedule

| Day | Activity | Platform Action |
|-----|----------|----------------|
| **Monday** | Post puzzle position (image + FEN) | All platforms simultaneously |
| **Tuesday** | Community discusses, early attempts posted | Engage with comments, give hints if needed |
| **Wednesday** | Optional hint posted if puzzle is Hard/Expert | Post hint as a follow-up comment |
| **Thursday** | Last call for submissions | Reminder post: "Answers close tomorrow!" |
| **Friday** | Reveal answer with full explanation video (3-5 min) | Upload video to YouTube/Bilibili, post summary to QQ/Discord |
| **Weekend** | Leaderboard update posted | Recognize top solvers |

### Posting Time
- **Chinese platforms (Bilibili, QQ):** Monday 8:00 AM CST (UTC+8)
- **English platforms (YouTube, Discord):** Monday 8:00 AM EST / 9:00 PM CST
- Stagger by 12 hours so each audience gets morning content

## Puzzle Difficulty Rotation (4-Week Cycle)

| Week | Difficulty | Target Audience | Expected Solve Rate |
|------|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Week 1 | Easy (初级) | Beginners, casual followers | 70-80% of participants |
| Week 2 | Medium (中级) | Intermediate players | 40-60% of participants |
| Week 3 | Hard (高级) | Advanced players | 15-30% of participants |
| Week 4 | Expert (专家) | Strong players, competitive challenge | 5-15% of participants |

### Difficulty Definitions
- **Easy:** Checkmate in 1-2 moves. All forcing moves. No significant defensive resources for the opponent.
- **Medium:** Checkmate or winning combination in 2-3 moves. May require one non-obvious move. Limited defensive options to consider.
- **Hard:** Checkmate or decisive advantage in 3-5 moves. Requires seeing a sacrifice or quiet move. Multiple defensive tries to refute.
- **Expert:** Complex combination of 4+ moves. May involve multiple sacrifices, quiet moves, or zugzwang. Defensive resources are real and must be calculated precisely.

## 4 Sample Puzzle Challenges

### Sample 1: Easy (初级) — Checkmate in 1

**Position Description:** Red has a chariot on the e-file aimed at the black general, and a cannon lined up with a cannon mount. One move ends the game.

**FEN:** `2bak4/4a4/4b4/9/9/4R4/9/4C4/4K4/9 w - - 0 1`

**Setup (plain language):**
- Red: General e2, Chariot e5, Cannon e3
- Black: General e9, Advisors d9+f10, Elephants c9+g8 (approximate — adjust to valid position)

**Solution:**
1. 炮五进六 (Cannon advances from e3 to e9)
- The cannon uses the chariot on e5 as the cannon mount (炮架) to capture/checkmate on e9
- The black general has no escape: advisors block the diagonal squares, and the cannon delivers mate

**Explanation points for Friday video:**
- This is the most basic cannon checkmate pattern
- Always look for a piece that can serve as a cannon mount in line with the enemy general
- The chariot does double duty: it is a threat on its own AND serves as the cannon's platform

---

### Sample 2: Medium (中级) — Checkmate in 2

**Position Description:** Red has a horse and cannon coordinated near the black palace. The classic 马后炮 (horse-cannon) pattern is available in two moves.

**FEN:** `3ak1N2/4a4/9/9/9/9/9/4C4/9/4K4 w - - 0 1`

**Setup:**
- Red: General e1, Cannon e3, Horse g9
- Black: General e9, Advisors d9+f10

**Solution:**
1. 马七退六 (Horse retreats from g9 to f7) — check!
   - The horse checks the general from f7. The general must move.
   - Black responds: 将5平6 (General moves from e9 to d9, or alternatively f9)

   If 将5平6 (General to d9):
2. 炮五平六 (Cannon shifts from e-file to d-file) — checkmate!
   - The cannon delivers check on the d-file, using the horse on f7 as a screen is not needed — the cannon lines up directly. The general is trapped by its own advisors.

   If 将5平4 (General to f9):
2. 炮五平四 (Cannon shifts to f-file) — checkmate!
   - Same pattern: cannon follows the general, horse controls the return square.

**Explanation points for Friday video:**
- 马后炮 is the most famous xiangqi checkmate pattern
- The horse controls the square the general wants to flee to
- The cannon delivers the final blow on the file/rank where the general sits
- Learn to recognize when you can force the general into a 马后炮 setup

### Sample 3: Hard (高级) — Winning Combination in 3 Moves

**Position Description:** Red sacrifices a chariot to set up a devastating discovered check, leading to material win or checkmate.

**FEN:** `2bak4/4a4/4b4/9/4r4/2R6/9/4B4/4A4/2BAK4 w - - 0 1`

**Setup:**
- Red: General e1, Chariot c5, Advisors e2+d1, Elephants e3+c1
- Black: General e9, Advisors d9+f10, Elephants c9+g8, Chariot e6

**Solution:**
1. 车三进四 (Chariot advances from c5 to c9) — sacrifice!
   - The chariot captures the elephant on c9, giving check via the exposed file. But Black can capture back.
   - Black responds: 仕4进5 (Advisor blocks or captures)

2. Depending on Black's response, Red follows up with a horse or cannon combination that exploits the weakened palace.

**Key idea:** The chariot sacrifice tears open the palace defenses. Even though Black can recapture, the structural damage to the palace allows a follow-up attack that wins decisively.

**Explanation points for Friday video:**
- Chariot sacrifices are the most spectacular combinations in xiangqi
- The purpose is not always checkmate — sometimes you sacrifice to destroy the palace structure
- After the sacrifice, count the remaining defenders. If the general has fewer than 2 defenders, a combination likely exists
- Calculate all of Black's defensive responses before committing to the sacrifice

### Sample 4: Expert (专家) — Complex Combination in 4+ Moves

**Position Description:** Red has a seemingly quiet position but launches a combination starting with a surprising cannon sacrifice, followed by horse maneuvers that weave through blocking pieces, culminating in an unavoidable checkmate.

**FEN:** `C1bak4/9/4b4/p3N4/9/9/9/4B4/4A4/2BAK4 w - - 0 1`

**Setup:**
- Red: General e1, Cannon a9, Horse e7, Advisors e2+d1, Elephants e3+c1
- Black: General e9, Advisors d9+f10, Elephants c9+g8, Pawn a7

**Solution:**
1. 炮九进一 (Cannon advances to check or threaten on the back rank)
   - Forces Black to respond to the cannon threat. Black has limited options.

   Black: 将5平4 (General moves to d9)

2. 马五进六 (Horse moves to f8) — check!
   - Horse checks from f8. The elephant on g8 might block but the horse path is clear.

   Black: 将4平5 (General returns to e9, forced)

3. 炮九平五 (Cannon swings to e-file)
   - Lines up cannon on the e-file with the general. Horse on f8 supports.

   Black: 仕6进5 (Advisor tries to block)

4. 马六退五 (Horse retreats to e6) — discovered check from cannon, and horse covers escape
   - Checkmate. The cannon checks on the e-file, the horse covers d8 and f8, and the advisor/elephant configuration leaves no escape.

**Explanation points for Friday video:**
- Expert puzzles require calculating 4+ moves with precision
- The opening cannon move looks quiet but sets up the entire combination
- The horse shuttles between squares, each time creating a new threat while maintaining the overall pattern
- Black's responses are all forced — that is the hallmark of a sound combination
- In your own games, look for positions where every opponent response is forced. If you can force 3-4 moves in a row, a winning combination almost certainly exists

## Puzzle Sourcing

### Where to Find Quality Puzzles
1. **Classic xiangqi puzzle collections** (排局谱) — centuries of compiled puzzles in traditional Chinese texts
2. **Personal game analysis** — extract critical moments from games analyzed on the channel
3. **Engine-assisted composition** — set up a thematic position and verify with Pikafish that exactly one line wins
4. **Community submissions** — invite community members to submit puzzles. Credit the submitter in the post. This increases engagement and content supply.
5. **Adapt from professional games** — take a critical moment from a pro game, simplify the position to isolate the key combination

### Quality Standards
- Every puzzle must have a single clearly winning line (verified by engine)
- The starting position must be realistic (could plausibly arise in a game)
- Easy and Medium puzzles should have forcing solutions (no major branches)
- Hard and Expert puzzles may have defensive tries that must be refuted
- All puzzles must be original compositions or properly credited adaptations
