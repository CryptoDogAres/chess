(Both are from Guangdong. Lu Qin is a few years older than Xu Yinchuan, became famous earlier, and plays with a fierce, fiery style — nicknamed "The Young Marshal of Guangzhou." 水与火的碰撞，从1990年代一直延续到2000年代。他们在全国赛、五羊杯、各种邀请赛上反复交手，每一次都万众瞩目。

2000年，两人又在全国赛中狭路相逢。吕钦想夺回冠军，许银川想证明自己才是这个时代的王者。
(In 2000, the two met again in the national championship. Lu Qin wanted to reclaim the title; Xu Yinchuan wanted to prove he was the king of this era.)

这盘棋，是他们宿命对决中最精彩的篇章之一。

## 总结与启示 Summary & Lessons (24:30-27:00)

好，我们来总结这盘棋的启示。
(Alright, let's summarize the lessons from this game.)

**第一，学会"连接手"。**
(First, learn "connecting moves.")
很多朋友下棋，每步棋都只想着一个目的。但高手下棋，一步棋至少有两三个功能。当你走了一步棋以后，想一想——我的其他棋子，因为这步棋变得更好了吗？如果答案是"是"，那就是好棋。
(Many players think about only one purpose per move. But masters think of at least two or three functions per move. After you make a move, ask yourself — have my other pieces improved because of this move? If the answer is "yes," that's a good move.)

**第二，弃子要有目的。**
(Second, sacrifices must have purpose.)
许银川弃炮不是乱来，他精确地计算了弃炮之后的每一步变化。弃子不是赌博——你不是在碰运气，你是在用一个子换取位置上的巨大优势。
(Xu Yinchuan's cannon sacrifice wasn't reckless — he precisely calculated every variation after the sacrifice. Sacrificing isn't gambling — you're not relying on luck, you're trading a piece for a massive positional advantage.)

**第三，"以退为进"是高级技巧。**
(Third, "advancing by retreating" is an advanced technique.)
我们很多人下棋都想着往前冲，觉得退就是认输。但许银川那步退马告诉我们——有时候退一步，反而到了最好的位置。人生也是这样，不是吗？
(Many of us want to charge forward when playing, thinking retreat means surrender. But Xu Yinchuan's retreating horse teaches us — sometimes stepping back brings you to the best position. Life is like that too, isn't it?)

**第四，了解你的对手。**
(Fourth, understand your opponent.)
许银川非常了解吕钦的风格，所以他在开局就选择了五七炮——一个既有攻击性又能保持稳定的阵型。面对不同的对手，要用不同的策略。
(Xu Yinchuan understood Lu Qin's style very well, so he chose the 57 Cannon in the opening — a formation that is both aggressive and stable. Against different opponents, use different strategies.)
