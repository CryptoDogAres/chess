## Position Setup (0:05-0:25)

"出自明朝古谱《适情雅趣》，这盘棋非常特别。红方：一个车在底线，三个兵散布在中间。黑方：也是三个卒加上黑将自己。总共就七个主力棋子——七星聚会的名字就是这么来的。"
(From the Ming Dynasty classic "Erta Elegantia" — this position is very special. Red: one chariot on the back rank, three pawns spread across the middle. Black: also three pawns plus the general. Seven key pieces total — that's where the name Seven Stars comes from.)

"看起来双方实力差不多，但实际上这盘棋暗藏杀机。红方先走，目标是和棋。没听错——红方要追求的是和棋！因为黑方的卒一旦冲到底线升变，红方就输了。"
(Looks like both sides are even, but deadly traps lurk beneath. Red moves first — and the goal is a draw. You heard right — Red is playing for a draw! Because if Black's pawns advance to the back rank, Red loses.)

## Bad Move #1 (0:35-0:55)

"很多人想：赶紧推兵！红方中兵往前拱一步，逼近黑将。听起来有道理？大错特错！你的兵往前推了一步，但黑方的卒也往前走一步。你推你的，我走我的——但黑方三个卒离底线更近，等黑卒冲下来，红方挡都挡不住。"
(Many think: push the pawns quick! Advance the center pawn toward the Black general. Sounds reasonable? Dead wrong! You push your pawn one step, but Black's pawn also advances one step. You do yours, I do mine — but Black's three pawns are closer to the back rank. When they arrive, Red can't stop them.)

"这个排局的精妙之处就在于：兵不是用来冲锋的，是用来配合车的！"
(The brilliance of this composition: the pawns aren't for charging — they're for coordinating with the chariot!)

## Bad Move #2 (0:55-1:15)

"那直接动车呢？车往前冲，吃掉一个黑卒？也不行！你吃了一个卒，但是车离开了底线，另外两个黑卒趁机冲下来。一个车追不上两个卒，你左右为难，最终还是输。"
(What about moving the chariot directly? Chariot charges forward, captures a Black pawn? Also wrong! You capture one pawn, but the chariot leaves the back rank, and the other two Black pawns rush down. One chariot can't chase two pawns — you're stuck, and you still lose.)

"贪吃子是大忌！"
(Greedy capturing is a cardinal sin!)

## Correct Move Reveal (1:15-2:00)

"正确的走法非常精妙——红方第一步，车平到中路！不吃子，不将军，就是安安静静地移到中间。为什么？因为车在中路可以同时监控黑方左右两边的卒！"
(The correct move is incredibly subtle — Red's first move: chariot slides to the center file! No capture, no check, just quietly shifting to the middle. Why? Because the chariot on the center file can simultaneously monitor Black's pawns on both sides!)

"黑方走卒往前拱。红方怎么办？车开始来回摆动，左边的卒要冲了就去左边挡，右边的卒要冲了就去右边拦。同时，红方的三个兵也在慢慢往前拱，一步一步逼近黑将。"
(Black advances a pawn. What does Red do? The chariot starts swinging back and forth — if the left pawn charges, block left; if the right pawn charges, intercept right. Meanwhile, Red's three pawns are slowly advancing, step by step approaching the Black general.)

"整个过程就像一场精密的舞蹈——车负责防守，兵负责进攻。黑方的卒想冲？红车拦住。黑将想跑？红兵逼过来。最终，红方利用车和兵的完美配合，形成长将或者长捉，逼和！"
(The whole process is like a precision dance — the chariot handles defense, the pawns handle offense. Black's pawns want to charge? Red chariot blocks. Black's general wants to run? Red pawns press forward. Finally, Red uses perfect chariot-pawn coordination to create perpetual check or perpetual chase — forced draw!)

"四百年了，这盘棋还是那么精彩。这就是七星聚会——棋局虽小，乾坤无限！"
(Four hundred years on, this puzzle is still breathtaking. This is Seven Stars Gathering — a small board, infinite universe!)

## Principle (2:00-2:20)

"七星聚会教给我们最重要的一课：全局观。不要只盯着一个点进攻，要统筹全局。车要灵活调动，兵要稳步推进，攻守要兼顾。下棋就像打仗——不是比谁冲得猛，是比谁的大局观更好。"
(Seven Stars Gathering teaches us the most important lesson: global vision. Don't fixate on one point of attack — coordinate the whole board. The chariot must be flexibly deployed, pawns must advance steadily, offense and defense must be balanced. Chess is like war — it's not about who charges hardest, but who sees the bigger picture.)
