## Bad Move #1 (0:30-0:50)

"最常见的错误：两个车一起从左边冲上去！一个车先进，另一个车跟在后面。看起来声势浩大，但问题是——两个车在同一条线上，只有前面那个车能发挥作用，后面那个车完全是浪费！"
(The most common mistake: both chariots charge up the left side together! One chariot advances, the other follows behind. Looks like a mighty force, but the problem — two chariots on the same line, only the front one is useful. The rear one is completely wasted!)

"两个车挤在一条线上，跟一个车有什么区别？白白浪费了一个大子！"
(Two chariots squeezed onto one line — how is that different from one chariot? A major piece completely wasted!)

## Correct Move Reveal (0:50-1:40)

"正确走法：后面那个车不要跟着前面的车走！后车平到右边去！车一平九——一下子从最左边跑到最右边。"
(Correct move: the rear chariot does NOT follow the front chariot! Rear chariot slides to the right side! Chariot shifts from the far left to the far right.)

"这就是'错'的意思——两个车要交错开，一个占左边，一个占右边。黑方顿时就头大了——你说我防左边还是防右边？"
(This is what 'interweave' means — the two chariots stagger, one on the left, one on the right. Black is immediately in trouble — defend left or defend right?)

"黑方先应付左边——出士挡住左边的车。好，红方右边的车冲上去！车九进七，直接杀到黑方底线，将军！黑将只能往左边躲。"
(Black deals with the left first — advisor moves to block the left chariot. Fine. Now Red's right chariot charges up! Chariot advances seven ranks, straight to Black's back rank, check! The general can only dodge left.)

"但左边的车早就等着了！左车也杀过来——车一进七，将军！黑将往右跑？右车等着呢。往左跑？左车等着呢。两个车轮番进攻，交替将军，黑方顾此失彼，最终无路可逃！"
(But the left chariot has been waiting! Left chariot also charges in — check! General runs right? Right chariot is waiting. Runs left? Left chariot is waiting. Two chariots alternating attacks, taking turns checking — Black can't cover both sides, and finally there's nowhere to run!)

"双车交替将军，一左一右，像剪刀一样把黑将夹在中间——绝杀！"
(Double chariots alternating checks, one left one right, like scissors trapping the Black general in the middle — checkmate!)

## Principle (1:40-1:55)

"双车错的核心原则是：子力要分散，不要集中。两个强子在一条线上就是浪费，分开在不同的线上才能形成多面夹击。打仗也是这个道理——不要把所有兵力集中在一个点上，要多路进攻，让对方顾此失彼。"
(The core principle of Double Chariot Interweave: spread your forces, don't concentrate them. Two strong pieces on one line is waste — split them across different lines to create multi-directional attacks. Same in war — don't concentrate all forces on one point. Attack from multiple directions so the opponent can't cover everything.)
