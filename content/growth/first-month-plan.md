## Posting Time Standards

All times are China Standard Time (CST / UTC+8) unless noted.

| Platform | Posting Time | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Douyin | 19:30 (7:30 PM) | Peak evening scroll window |
| Bilibili | 17:30 (5:30 PM) | Pre-dinner viewing; algorithm indexes before evening surge |
| YouTube | 17:00 EST (next day) | North American afternoon; best for English-language xiangqi audience |

## Week 1 Goals: Realistic Benchmarks for a New Channel

These are realistic targets for a brand-new channel with zero existing audience and no paid promotion. Adjust expectations up if you have an existing following on other platforms.

### Douyin (most achievable due to algorithmic distribution to new accounts)

| Metric | Week 1 Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Total views (all 4 shorts) | 2,000-8,000 | Douyin gives new videos a small test pool of 200-500 viewers; strong completion rate can push to thousands |
| Followers gained | 20-80 | Focus on follow-worthy content, not follower count |
| Average completion rate | 40%+ | This is the metric that matters most; above 50% is excellent |
| Comments | 10-30 total | Actively reply to every comment to boost engagement signals |
| Best-case scenario | One video hits 10,000+ views | Happens when completion rate exceeds 60% and the algorithm escalates distribution |

### Bilibili

| Metric | Week 1 Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Total views (2 long-form + 1 series) | 500-2,000 | Bilibili is slower to distribute to new creators; quality compounds over time |
| Followers gained | 10-40 | Bilibili followers are more committed; lower number but higher value |
| 三连 (like + coin + favorite) | 5-20 per video | Coins and favorites are strong signals to Bilibili's algorithm |
| Comments | 5-15 total | Quality of comments matters; long thoughtful replies signal engaged audience |
| 弹幕 (bullet comments) | Any at all is a win | Bullet comments indicate real-time engagement; even 2-3 per video is a positive sign |

### YouTube

| Metric | Week 1 Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Total views (2 long-form + 1 series + shorts) | 100-500 | YouTube is the slowest for new channels; search traffic takes weeks to build |
| Subscribers gained | 5-20 | YouTube growth is back-loaded; focus on searchable evergreen content |
| Average view duration | 40%+ of video length | The most important metric; YouTube pushes videos that retain viewers |
| Click-through rate | 4-8% | Measured once YouTube shows your video in recommendations (may take a few days) |
| Shorts views | 200-2,000 | YouTube Shorts have their own discovery feed and can outperform long-form initially |

### Cross-Platform Week 1 Goals

- Reply to 100% of comments across all platforms within 24 hours.
- Post at least one community/dynamic post per platform beyond the videos.
- Have all Week 2 videos filmed and in editing by Sunday night.
- Identify which of the 4 puzzle shorts had the highest completion rate and note what made it different.

### Month 1 Cumulative Targets

| Metric | Douyin | Bilibili | YouTube |
|---|---|---|---|
| Followers | 100-500 | 50-200 | 20-100 |
| Total views | 10,000-50,000 | 3,000-15,000 | 1,000-5,000 |
| Videos with 1,000+ views | 2-5 | 1-3 | 0-2 |
| Average completion/retention | 40%+ | 40%+ | 35%+ |

These are not vanity metrics to celebrate. They are diagnostic numbers. If Douyin completion rate is below 30%, the hooks need reworking. If Bilibili coin rate is near zero, the content is not reaching the right audience. If YouTube CTR is below 3%, thumbnails and titles need A/B testing. Track everything in the spreadsheet described in `content/platform-guides/cross-platform-strategy.md` (Section 5).
