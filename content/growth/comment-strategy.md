# Comment Reply Strategy

Comments are not an afterthought — they are a growth engine. Platform algorithms on Douyin, Bilibili, and YouTube all use comment velocity (how fast comments appear) and comment depth (replies, threads) as signals to push content to more viewers. A video with 50 comments in the first hour will outperform a video with 200 comments that trickle in over a week.

This guide covers how to maximize engagement through strategic comment management across all three platforms.

---

## Rule #1: Speed Matters

**Reply within 2 hours of posting.** The first two hours after a video goes live are the critical window when platforms decide whether to push it to a wider audience.

### The Math
- A reply to every comment effectively doubles your comment count (their comment + your reply).
- Each reply triggers a notification, pulling the commenter back to your video — which counts as a return visit and signals the algorithm that the content is engaging.
- Estimated reach boost from active early replies: 15-20% on Douyin, 10-15% on Bilibili, 10-20% on YouTube.

### Practical System
1. **Publish the video and immediately set a 2-hour timer.**
2. **Stay on the platform for the first 30 minutes.** Reply to every comment in real time.
3. **Check back at the 1-hour and 2-hour marks.** Reply to all new comments.
4. **After 2 hours**: reply within 24 hours. Speed is less critical now, but consistency still matters.
5. **Never leave a genuine comment without a reply.** Every unanswered comment is a missed connection.

## Rule #2: Reply Templates for Common Comment Types

These templates are starting points. Personalize every reply — viewers can spot copy-pasted responses instantly.

### "太厉害了！" / "好强！" / "学到了！" (Viewer Praise)

**Strategy**: Thank them genuinely, then ask a follow-up question to keep the conversation going. A question in your reply increases the chance of a second comment, which deepens the thread.

**Templates**:
- "谢谢！你觉得这步棋最难看出来的点在哪里？"
- "哈哈谢谢支持！你平时下棋遇到过这种局面吗？"
- "谢谢！下一期准备讲[话题]，你觉得这个方向对吗？"

**Avoid**: "谢谢！" alone. A one-word reply is worse than no reply — it signals low effort.

### "我觉得应该走..." / "第X步不如走..." (Viewer Suggests a Move)

**Strategy**: This is gold. The viewer is engaged deeply enough to analyze the position. Validate their thinking, then explain the nuance. This creates an educational micro-moment in the comments that other viewers will read.

**Templates**:
- "好想法！走[他们的建议]确实看起来很自然，但是对方可以[具体应对]，这样反而会[后果]。不过你的思路方向是对的！"
- "这步确实也可以！其实走[他们的建议]和我讲的走法各有优劣，你说的走法更稳健，我讲的更激进。取决于棋风！"
- "你说的没错，这步在[某个变化]下确实是更好的选择。我视频里没展开讲这个变化，下次可以专门分析一下！"

**Avoid**: Never dismiss a viewer's analysis with "不对" or "你看错了." Even if they are wrong, frame it as "有道理，但是..." or "这个角度很好，不过..."

### "看不懂" / "太难了" / "跟不上" (Viewer Confused)

**Strategy**: These comments are feedback — your content may be too advanced or your explanation too fast. Respond warmly, simplify the key point, and direct them to beginner content.

**Templates**:
- "没关系！这个局面确实比较复杂。简单来说就是[一句话简化版]。如果想从基础开始可以看看我的新手教程系列！"
- "慢慢来！建议先看我的[具体教程编号]，那期讲的[基础概念]是理解这盘棋的基础。"
- "这步的关键就一个字：[核心概念]。掌握了这个概念再回来看这段就清楚了！"

**Avoid**: Never make the viewer feel stupid. Never say "这个很简单啊" or "你应该知道的."

### "第X步为什么不走..." / "这步分析有问题吧？" (Viewer Challenges Analysis)

**Strategy**: This is the most important comment type to handle well. A respectful, detailed response to a challenge demonstrates expertise and intellectual honesty. Other viewers are watching how you handle disagreement.

**Templates**:
- "好问题！走[他们的建议]确实看起来不错，但是仔细算下去会出现[具体变化]，最终[结果]。不过你提的这个变化确实值得深入讨论！"
- "你说得对，这步确实有争议！实际上[棋手名]在赛后也提到过他考虑了你说的走法，但最终因为[原因]选择了视频里的走法。两种都有道理。"
- "感谢指正！我回去用软件验证了一下，[他们的建议]确实在某些变化下更优。下期视频我会做个补充说明。"

**Avoid**: Never be defensive. If you are wrong, admit it quickly and gracefully — it builds more credibility than being right. Never say "你不懂" or "我是对的."

### "求更多残局！" / "能不能讲讲[话题]？" (Content Request)

**Strategy**: Content requests tell you what your audience wants. Acknowledge the request, give a timeline if possible, and use it as a teaser for upcoming content.

**Templates**:
- "收到！残局系列每周一三五更新，下一期[日期]就有新的！"
- "好主意！[话题]确实很多人想看，我已经在准备了，大概[时间范围]能出！"
- "这个话题太好了，加到选题列表了！如果点赞过[数字]我就优先做这个！"

**Avoid**: Never promise a specific date unless you are certain. "下周出" followed by silence is worse than "近期会做."

### Trolls / Negativity / Spam

**Strategy**: Do not feed trolls. The goal is to keep your comment section positive without creating drama that discourages genuine commenters.

**Decision tree**:
1. **Factual error disguised as criticism** ("这步明显错了你不会下棋吧"): Brief, factual correction. "走[具体走法]之后对方可以[具体应对]，所以视频里的走法更优。可以用软件验证。" Then move on.
2. **Pure insult with no chess content** ("垃圾视频"): Ignore completely. Do not reply, do not delete (deleting can create more drama if the troll screenshots). It will be buried by positive comments.
3. **Spam / ads**: Delete and block. No engagement.
4. **Constructive criticism with rude tone** ("讲得太慢了能不能快点"): Extract the feedback, ignore the tone. "感谢反馈！我会在后续视频里注意节奏。如果觉得慢可以用1.5倍速看！"

**Never**: Argue, insult back, be sarcastic, or make passive-aggressive replies. Your reply represents your brand to every viewer who reads it.

## Rule #3: Engagement Boosters

Proactive techniques to drive more comments, not just respond to them.

### Pin a Question as First Comment

Immediately after publishing, post and pin a comment with a question. This primes viewers to comment — they see a question and feel invited to respond.

**Templates**:
- "你猜对了吗？评论区告诉我你的答案！"
- "这盘棋你觉得最关键的一步是第几步？"
- "如果你是红方，第15步你会怎么走？"
- "今天的残局你用了多长时间解出来？"
- "你觉得这个开局适合新手还是高手？"

**Rules**:
- One pinned question per video, no more.
- The question should be answerable by anyone who watched the video, not just experts.
- Rotate between opinion questions ("你觉得...") and factual challenges ("你猜对了吗...").

### Reply with a Follow-Up Puzzle in Comments

After the video has been live for 4-6 hours and initial engagement has settled, post a bonus puzzle in the comments.

**Template**:
- "看完视频的同学来个加餐：[描述一个简单的相关残局]。答案明天公布！"

This does three things: gives returning viewers new content, creates a reason to check back tomorrow, and generates a second wave of comment activity.

### Heart / Like Every Genuine Comment

On YouTube, hearting a comment sends a notification to the commenter. On Bilibili, liking ("点赞") a comment pushes it higher in the comment section. On Douyin, liking serves a similar function.

**Rule**: Heart or like every comment that is genuine — even simple ones like "好看." The notification brings them back. The visible heart/like signals to other viewers that you are active and engaged.

**Time budget**: 5 minutes per video per day for the first 3 days after publishing. After that, check once daily.

## Rule #4: Platform-Specific Tips

### Douyin (抖音)

**Video Reply (视频回复)**
Douyin allows you to reply to a comment with a short video. This is extremely powerful:
- It creates new content (the reply video appears in your feed).
- It flatters the commenter (their comment is featured).
- It triggers Douyin's algorithm as both a comment interaction AND new content.

**When to use video replies**:
- When a viewer asks a question that requires showing the board ("第8步之后怎么走？")
- When a viewer suggests an alternative move that you can analyze quickly
- When a comment is funny or insightful enough to feature

**Frequency**: 1-2 video replies per week. More than that dilutes your main content feed.

**Douyin Comment Section Culture**:
- Comments are sorted by likes, not chronology. A well-liked pinned comment stays visible permanently.
- Viewer debates in comments boost the video. If two commenters are respectfully arguing about a move, do not intervene immediately — let the thread develop, then weigh in after a few exchanges.
- Use 表情包 (emoji/stickers) sparingly in replies to match the playful tone of Douyin.

### Bilibili (B站)

**Danmu Culture (弹幕)**
Bilibili's scrolling on-screen comments (弹幕) are a unique engagement layer. While you cannot directly control danmu, you can encourage it:
- At key moments in your video, pause and say "弹幕里告诉我你的答案" or "在弹幕上打出你猜的走法."
- This creates visible on-screen activity that makes the video feel alive to new viewers.

**Pinned Comments (置顶评论)**
Bilibili allows pinning your own comment. Use this for:
- The question prompt (same as above).
- Corrections: if you made an error in the video, pin a correction rather than re-uploading. Honesty builds trust.
- Links to related videos in your channel.

**Zhuanlan Follow-Ups (专栏)**
Bilibili's article feature (专栏) allows you to post written content. Use this for:
- Extended analysis that did not fit in the video.
- Written move-by-move breakdowns that viewers can study at their own pace.
- Behind-the-scenes content about how you prepare videos.
- Link the 专栏 article in your video's pinned comment: "详细棋谱和变化分析在专栏文章里，链接在置顶评论！"

**Bilibili Engagement Features**:
- Encourage 一键三连 (like + coin + favorite) in every video, but do it naturally ("如果觉得有帮助，三连支持一下！").
- Reply to comments with your Bilibili username visible — viewers who see you active are more likely to follow.

### YouTube

**Heart Comments**
YouTube's heart feature sends a push notification to the commenter's phone. This is the single most effective re-engagement tool on the platform.
- Heart every genuine comment within the first 24 hours.
- Heart interesting comments from weeks-old videos when you see them — the notification pulls the viewer back.

**Pin Best Comment**
Pin the most insightful or entertaining viewer comment (not your own question — use your own pinned comment for the first 24 hours, then switch to a viewer comment after engagement settles).
- Pinning a viewer's comment makes them feel valued and signals to other viewers that good comments get recognized.

**Community Posts**
YouTube Community tab allows text, image, and poll posts between video uploads:
- Post a poll before a puzzle video: "今天的残局你觉得几步能解？" with options like "3步", "5步", "7步以上."
- Post a board position image with "明天的视频分析这盘棋，你觉得红方该怎么走？"
- Post milestones: "感谢100位订阅者！" (these perform surprisingly well for small channels).

**YouTube Shorts Comments**:
- Shorts comments behave differently — they are fast-scrolling and less likely to generate deep threads.
- Focus on quick, punchy replies for Shorts.
- Save detailed analytical replies for long-form video comments.

## Daily Comment Routine

| Time | Action | Duration |
|------|--------|----------|
| Within 30 min of posting | Reply to all early comments, post pinned question | 15 min |
| 1 hour after posting | Second round of replies | 10 min |
| 2 hours after posting | Third round of replies, heart/like all genuine comments | 10 min |
| Evening check | Reply to afternoon/evening comments on today's video | 15 min |
| Evening check | Reply to new comments on older videos (past 7 days) | 10 min |
| **Total daily** | | **~60 min** |

This one hour per day is non-negotiable for growth. It is as important as the content itself. A channel that posts great videos but ignores comments will grow slower than a channel that posts good videos and engages aggressively.

## Comment Metrics to Track

Review these weekly to measure engagement health:

| Metric | Target (Month 1-3) | Target (Month 4-6) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Comments per video | 10-20 | 30-50 |
| Reply rate (your replies / total comments) | 90-100% | 70-80% |
| Average reply time (first comment) | Under 30 min | Under 1 hour |
| Comment-to-view ratio | 1-2% | 2-3% |
| Pinned question response rate | 5-10 replies | 15-30 replies |
| Video replies per week (Douyin) | 1-2 | 2-3 |
| Community posts per week (YouTube) | 2-3 | 3-5 |

If comment counts are low, the issue is usually one of three things:
1. **No pinned question**: Viewers need an invitation to comment.
2. **Slow reply time**: Early commenters do not get the dopamine hit of a reply, so they stop commenting.
3. **Content does not provoke opinion**: Add a "what would you do?" moment to every video.

---

## The Golden Rule

Every comment is a person who chose to spend their time engaging with your content. Treat each one as the beginning of a relationship, not a metric to optimize. The creators who build lasting audiences are the ones whose comment sections feel like a conversation, not a performance.
