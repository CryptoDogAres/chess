# 10 Xiangqi Creators to Engage With

Building an audience from zero requires strategic engagement with existing creators. This document identifies 10 xiangqi creators across Douyin, Bilibili, and YouTube, organized into three tiers based on audience size and collaboration potential.

The goal is not to spam or leech — it is to become a recognized, thoughtful presence in the xiangqi content community so that when viewers see our name in comments, they click through to our channel.

---

## Tier 1 — Large Creators (100K+ Followers)

These creators are too large to collaborate with directly at our stage. The strategy is pure engagement: leave thoughtful comments that other viewers upvote, making our name visible.

### 1. 许银川 (Xu Yinchuan)

- **Handle**: @许银川象棋
- **Platform**: Douyin
- **Estimated followers**: 500K+
- **Content style**: Short-form game commentary from a legendary grandmaster. Relaxed, authoritative tone. Posts highlights from his career and analysis of classic games. Videos are typically 1-3 minutes.
- **Why engage**: Xu Yinchuan is one of the most respected names in xiangqi history. His comment section attracts serious fans — exactly our target audience. A well-upvoted comment under his videos puts our name in front of thousands of engaged xiangqi enthusiasts.
- **How to approach**: Comment with specific analytical observations, not generic praise. Example: "许老师这步弃车妙在控制了黑方的出路，如果黑方走士5退4反而加速败局。精彩！" Never self-promote. Never say "come check out my channel." Just be the smartest comment in the room, consistently.

## Comment Strategy Templates

### What to Write

**Analytical comments** (best for Tier 1 channels):
- "这步[具体走法]妙在[具体原因]，如果改走[替代走法]反而会[具体后果]。"
- "这个局面让我想起[某某年某某比赛]，当时[棋手名]走了不同的选择，结果..."
- "请问老师，如果黑方在第X步改走[具体走法]，是不是有[具体变化]的可能？"

**Supportive comments** (best for Tier 2-3 channels):
- "讲得很清楚！这个[具体知识点]我之前一直搞不懂，看了你的讲解终于明白了。"
- "补充一点：这个局面在实战中经常出现在[具体开局]之后，很实用！"
- "期待你的下一期！能不能讲讲[相关话题]？"

**Community-building comments** (best for all tiers):
- Reply to other viewers' questions with helpful answers.
- "@[其他评论者] 你说的没错，这步确实还有另一个变化..."
- Share your own attempt at solving a puzzle before reading other answers.

### What NOT to Write

- **Never self-promote directly**: "我的频道也有类似内容" is transparent and will be ignored or resented.
- **Never copy their analysis**: If you plan to cover the same game, add a genuinely different perspective.
- **Never criticize to show off**: "老师这步分析错了" without respectful framing burns bridges.
- **Never post generic comments**: "好棒！" "厉害！" "支持！" These are invisible in a sea of identical comments.
- **Never comment and disappear**: Consistency matters more than any single brilliant comment. Show up every week.

## Content Collaboration Ideas

### Joint Analysis (联合解说)
Both channels analyze the same famous game independently, then each recommends the other's video at the end. Viewers get two perspectives and discover a new channel. Low effort — no scheduling required.

### Challenge Match (友谊对弈)
Play a casual game against the other creator. Record with dual commentary (each records their own perspective). Great for Douyin short-form cuts and YouTube long-form.

### Guest Commentary (客座解说)
Appear as a guest on their video or invite them onto yours. Works especially well for tournament coverage — two voices analyzing a live game is more engaging than one.

### Puzzle Exchange (残局交换)
Create a puzzle for their audience, they create one for yours. Each channel posts it with credit and a recommendation to check out the other channel.

### Series Cross-Over (系列联动)
If both channels have ongoing series, do a crossover episode. Example: if they teach openings and you teach endgames, do an episode that follows one game from opening through endgame, each covering your specialty.

### Live Stream Collaboration (联合直播)
Co-host a live stream on Douyin or Bilibili. Analyze viewer-submitted games together, or play and commentate a blitz session. Live formats build personal connection faster than edited videos.
