# 7-Video Weekly Posting Schedule

A complete weekly content calendar for building a xiangqi channel across Douyin, Bilibili, and YouTube. This schedule is designed to be sustainable for a solo creator while maintaining the consistency needed to trigger platform algorithms.

The core principle: three short-form puzzle videos for reach, two long-form educational videos for depth, one series episode for retention, and one live stream for community.

## Optimal Posting Times

Posting times are based on peak activity patterns for Chinese-language content consumers. Adjust based on your own analytics after the first month.

### Douyin (Short-Form Puzzles)
- **Monday/Wednesday/Friday**: 12:00-12:30 PM CST (lunch break traffic)
- **Alternative slot**: 7:30-8:00 PM CST (evening scroll)
- **Why**: Douyin traffic peaks during commute and lunch hours. Short puzzle content fits the "quick break" usage pattern.

### Bilibili (Long-Form Educational)
- **Tuesday (Game Analysis)**: 6:00-7:00 PM CST (early evening, viewers settle in for longer content)
- **Thursday (Tutorial)**: 7:00-8:00 PM CST (slightly later, study-mode viewers)
- **Saturday (Series)**: 10:00-11:00 AM CST (weekend morning, relaxed viewing)
- **Why**: Bilibili's audience watches longer content in the evening and on weekends. Educational content performs best when viewers have time to focus.

### YouTube (Cross-Posted)
- **Shorts (same day as Douyin)**: 8:00-9:00 AM PST / 12:00-1:00 AM CST+1 (catches North American morning + late-night Asian viewers)
- **Long-form (same day as Bilibili)**: 5:00-6:00 PM PST / 9:00-10:00 AM CST+1 (North American evening)
- **Why**: YouTube's primary xiangqi audience outside China is in North America (overseas Chinese communities) and Southeast Asia. Posting for PST evening covers both time zones reasonably.

### Sunday Live Stream
- **Douyin**: 7:30-10:00 PM CST (prime time for live content)
- **Bilibili**: Simulcast if possible, or alternate weeks between platforms
- **Why**: Sunday evening is peak leisure time. Viewers are relaxed and willing to spend 1-2 hours watching and chatting.

## Cross-Posting Adaptation Checklist

Not every video can be uploaded identically across platforms. Use this checklist when adapting content.

### Douyin to YouTube Shorts
- [ ] Re-export in 9:16 vertical format if not already
- [ ] Add English subtitles or bilingual captions for overseas audience
- [ ] Adjust title to include English keywords: "Xiangqi Puzzle #X — Can You Solve It?"
- [ ] Add relevant English hashtags: #xiangqi #chinesechess #puzzle
- [ ] Thumbnail: same as Douyin but with English text overlay

### Bilibili to YouTube (Long-Form)
- [ ] Re-export with bilingual subtitles (Chinese hardcoded + English .srt upload)
- [ ] Adjust title format: "中文标题 | English Title"
- [ ] Write YouTube description with timestamps, English summary, and links
- [ ] Create YouTube-specific thumbnail (larger text, brighter colors — YouTube thumbnails are viewed at smaller sizes)
- [ ] Add end screen linking to next video and subscribe prompt
- [ ] Add cards linking to related videos at key moments
- [ ] Check that any Bilibili-specific references ("一键三连", "投币") are removed or replaced

### General Cross-Posting Rules
- [ ] Never mention a competing platform by name in the video itself
- [ ] Platform-specific calls to action recorded as separate clips and spliced in during editing
- [ ] Watermark your original content but do not use another platform's watermark
- [ ] Upload natively to each platform — never share a link to another platform's video

## Batch Recording Strategy

Recording one video at a time is inefficient. Batch recording saves setup time, maintains consistent energy, and builds a content buffer.

### Puzzle Videos (Batch of 5)
- **Time needed**: 1.5-2 hours total
- **Process**:
  1. Set up board, camera, lighting, and microphone once
  2. Record 5 puzzle presentations back-to-back
  3. Between each puzzle: reset board, take a 2-minute break, review the next script
  4. Do NOT change clothes between recordings (unless you want variety — in that case, change shirts only and record in sets of 2-3)
- **Schedule**: Record every other Sunday afternoon. This creates a 10-day buffer of puzzle content.

### Long-Form Videos (Batch of 2)
- **Time needed**: 3-4 hours total
- **Process**:
  1. Record two long-form videos in one session (e.g., one game analysis + one tutorial)
  2. Take a 15-minute break between recordings
  3. Different content types help maintain energy — switching from analysis to teaching resets your focus
- **Schedule**: Record every Saturday morning. Edit throughout the following week.

### Series Episodes (Batch of 2-3)
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours total
- **Process**: Series episodes have consistent format, making them ideal for batching. Record 2-3 at once.
- **Schedule**: Record once per month to maintain a 2-3 week buffer.

## Content Pipeline

A structured pipeline prevents the "what do I record today?" paralysis that kills consistency.

### Weekly Pipeline Flow

```
Week N (Planning):
  Monday    — Write scripts for Week N+1 long-form videos
  Tuesday   — Research and outline Week N+1 puzzle scripts
  Wednesday — Finalize all scripts, prepare board positions
  Thursday  — Record long-form batch (2 videos)
  Friday    — Record puzzle batch (3-5 videos)
  Saturday  — Edit Tuesday's video (publish next Tuesday)
  Sunday    — Edit Thursday's video (publish next Thursday)

Week N+1 (Publishing):
  Monday    — Publish Puzzle, edit Saturday's series episode
  Tuesday   — Publish Game Analysis
  Wednesday — Publish Puzzle
  Thursday  — Publish Tutorial
  Friday    — Publish Puzzle
  Saturday  — Publish Series Episode
  Sunday    — Live Stream + begin planning Week N+2
```

### Key Pipeline Rules
1. **Scripts are written at least 5 days before publish date.** No same-day writing and recording.
2. **Recording happens in batches, not daily.** Two recording sessions per week maximum.
3. **Editing is spread across the week.** One video edited per day keeps it manageable.
4. **Sunday is dual-purpose**: live stream in the evening, planning for the next week in the morning.
5. **Never publish unedited content** (except live streams). Even puzzle videos need title cards, music, and a clean cut.

## Buffer Content: 3 Emergency Videos

Pre-record these videos and keep them edited and ready to publish. They cover evergreen topics that are relevant regardless of when they go live.

### Emergency Video 1: 象棋历史：从古代到现代的演变
- **Type**: Series / Documentary style
- **Duration**: 8-10 minutes
- **Why it works as backup**: Timeless content with no expiration date. Cultural/historical angle appeals broadly.
- **Platform**: Bilibili + YouTube

### Emergency Video 2: 残局谜题合集：5个经典残局一次看完
- **Type**: Puzzle compilation
- **Duration**: 5-8 minutes
- **Why it works as backup**: Compilation of previously unused puzzles. Can fill any content slot. Works on all platforms.
- **Platform**: All platforms (cut into individual shorts if needed for Douyin)

### Emergency Video 3: 我为什么开始做象棋视频？
- **Type**: Personal / vlog style
- **Duration**: 5-7 minutes
- **Why it works as backup**: Personal story never expires. Builds connection with audience. Can be posted whenever engagement dips or you need a break from instructional content.
- **Platform**: Bilibili + YouTube

### Buffer Management Rules
1. **Always maintain 3 buffer videos.** When you use one, record a replacement within the next recording session.
2. **Rotate buffer content quarterly.** If a buffer video has not been used in 3 months, publish it and record a fresh replacement.
3. **Buffer videos should require zero additional editing.** They must be publish-ready — rendered, with thumbnails and descriptions pre-written.
4. **Store buffer video files, thumbnails, and descriptions in**: `content/buffer/` with clear naming: `buffer-01-history.mp4`, `buffer-01-thumbnail.png`, `buffer-01-description.txt`.

| Task | Hours/Week |
|------|-----------|
| Script writing | 4-5 |

| Editing (5-6 videos) | 8-10 |

| Publishing + cross-posting | 1-2 |
| Community engagement (comments) | 3-4 |

| **Total** | **27-36** |

This is a part-time-to-full-time commitment. The first month will feel heavy as you establish workflows. By month two, templates and batch processes cut the time significantly. By month three, the pipeline runs semi-automatically and the main time investment is creative — writing scripts and engaging with the community.
