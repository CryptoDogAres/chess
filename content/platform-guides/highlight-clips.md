# Creating Highlight Clips from Full Games

A guide for cutting short Douyin clips and YouTube Shorts from long-form Bilibili and YouTube game analysis videos. Turn one 20-minute analysis into 3-5 short clips that drive traffic back to the full video.

---

## Why Create Highlight Clips

Long-form game analysis is the backbone of your Bilibili and YouTube content. It showcases your depth of knowledge and builds authority. But the reality is that most viewers will never find a 20-minute video through algorithmic recommendation. Short-form clips solve this problem.

A highlight clip works like a movie trailer. It shows the most exciting 30-60 seconds of your analysis, hooks the viewer, and directs them to the full video. This creates a content funnel:

**Short clip (Douyin/Shorts) → Curiosity → Full video (Bilibili/YouTube) → Subscribe → Loyalty**

Every full game analysis you produce should generate at least 2-3 highlight clips. This is not extra work. It is extracting maximum value from work you have already done.

---

## 1. What Makes a Good Clip

Not every moment in a chess analysis makes a good clip. You are looking for moments with inherent dramatic tension that can stand alone without 10 minutes of context.

### The Five Types of Clip-Worthy Moments

**Type 1: Big Evaluation Swing**
The position looks equal or losing, then one move changes everything. The engine evaluation bar swings from -2 to +5. This visual drama is immediately compelling.
- Example: "Red was losing the entire game. Then this one move changed everything."
- Visual cue: Show the eval bar swinging dramatically.

**Type 2: Sacrifice**
Any move where a piece is given up for a non-obvious advantage. Sacrifices are inherently exciting because they violate the basic rule of "do not lose material."
- Example: A rook sacrifice that leads to a mating attack three moves later.
- Visual cue: Zoom into the sacrificed piece, slow the moment down.

**Type 3: Unexpected Move**
A move that looks wrong at first glance but turns out to be brilliant. This creates a satisfying "aha" moment.
- Example: Moving the king forward in the middle game, which turns out to be the only winning move.
- Visual cue: Pause before revealing the move, let the viewer try to guess.

**Type 4: Checkmate Sequence**
The final combination that ends the game. Clean, forced checkmate sequences are the most shareable type of clip.
- Example: A four-move forced checkmate with a double cannon setup.
- Visual cue: Show each move of the sequence with brief pauses.

**Type 5: Critical Error**
A moment where one player makes a losing mistake, especially if the correct move was subtle. This is educational and dramatic.
- Example: "Black played chariot to e5. This looks natural, but it loses immediately. Can you see why?"
- Visual cue: Show the losing move, then reveal the refutation.

### Quick Test for Clip Quality

Before cutting a clip, ask yourself:
1. Would this moment make someone stop scrolling? If not, skip it.
2. Can a viewer understand the drama without watching the full game? If not, add context.
3. Does it end with the viewer wanting to know more? If so, perfect.

## 2. Clip Length Guidelines

| Platform | Ideal Length | Maximum Length | Notes |
|----------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Douyin | 30-45 seconds | 60 seconds | Shorter is almost always better on Douyin |
| YouTube Shorts | 40-58 seconds | 59 seconds | YouTube Shorts can be slightly longer |
| Bilibili (short video) | 30-60 seconds | 3 minutes | Bilibili is more forgiving on length |

### The 45-Second Sweet Spot

For maximum cross-platform compatibility, aim for 45 seconds. This length works well on all three platforms, gives you enough time to set up the context and deliver the payoff, and keeps the completion rate high.

**Time budget within 45 seconds**:
- 0-5 seconds: Hook (the dramatic moment or question)
- 5-15 seconds: Minimal context (set up the position)
- 15-35 seconds: The key sequence of moves
- 35-42 seconds: Reaction or explanation of why this matters
- 42-45 seconds: CTA (link to full video)

## 3. Clip Structure

Every highlight clip should follow this four-part structure. This is not optional. Clips that skip the hook or the CTA underperform consistently.

### Part 1: Jump Straight to the Critical Position (0-5 seconds)

Do NOT start with "Hi everyone, today we are looking at a game between..." That intro kills your clip. Start with the board already showing the critical position.

**Effective openings**:
- Show the board position and say: "红方一步弃车，直接翻盘" (Red sacrifices the rook and turns the game around)
- Start with the eval bar showing a big swing
- Open with the question: "这步棋你会怎么走？" (What would you play here?)
- Show the checkmate position first, then rewind: "我们倒回去看看怎么走到这一步的" (Let's rewind and see how we got here)

The first frame of your video must be visually interesting. A board position with pieces in tension is visually interesting. A logo or intro animation is not.

### Part 2: Show the Move Sequence (5-35 seconds)

This is the core of the clip. Walk through the key moves with brief commentary.

**Pacing guidelines**:
- One move every 3-5 seconds. Do not rush.
- Pause for 1-2 seconds before the critical move to build anticipation.
- Use visual cues: arrows showing the move, circles highlighting the key square, zoom on the piece being sacrificed.
- Keep verbal commentary short and punchy. One sentence per move maximum.
- If the sequence is longer than 5 moves, consider showing only the most dramatic 3-4 moves and summarizing the rest.

### Part 3: Reaction and Explanation (35-42 seconds)

After the sequence plays out, add your reaction or a brief explanation of the significance.

**Options**:
- Your genuine reaction: "太精彩了" (Amazing), "这步棋太妙了" (What a brilliant move)
- Engine evaluation: "引擎评估从-3直接到+8" (Engine eval went from -3 straight to +8)
- Historical context: "这是1982年全国冠军赛的决胜局" (This was the decisive game of the 1982 national championship)
- Educational takeaway: "记住这个马的位置，这是经典的闷宫杀法" (Remember this knight placement, this is the classic smothered mate pattern)

### Part 4: CTA — Drive Traffic to Full Video (42-45 seconds)

The last 3 seconds should direct viewers to the full analysis. This is the entire point of creating highlight clips.

**CTA options (pick one)**:
- Text overlay: "完整解说在B站/YouTube" (Full analysis on Bilibili/YouTube)
- Verbal: "完整对局解说看置顶视频" (Full game analysis in the pinned video)
- Text overlay with your channel name and an arrow pointing to your profile
- End card: "想看完整分析？关注后看主页" (Want the full analysis? Follow and check my profile)

**Important**: On Douyin, you cannot add clickable links in videos. The best you can do is direct people to your profile, where your Bilibili/YouTube link should be in your bio. On YouTube Shorts, you can add the link to the full video in the description.

## 4. Editing Workflow

### During the Full Game Recording

The most efficient time to plan your clips is while recording the full analysis. Do not wait until editing.

1. **Keep a timestamp log**: While recording or immediately after, note timestamps where clip-worthy moments happen. Use a simple format:
   - 3:42 — Rook sacrifice, eval swing
   - 8:15 — Unexpected knight retreat
   - 14:30 — Checkmate sequence starts
   - 17:05 — Critical mistake by black

2. **Mark at least 3-5 potential clip moments per full game**. You will not use all of them, but having options is better than re-watching the entire video later.

### Clip Editing Steps

**Step 1: Export the Segment**

Open your full video in your editing software (剪映/CapCut, DaVinci Resolve, Premiere, or similar).
- Go to the timestamp you noted
- Set in-point 5-10 seconds before the key moment (you need context)
- Set out-point when the sequence resolves
- Export this segment as a separate file

**Step 2: Add Vertical Crop (if needed)**

If your full game analysis was recorded in 16:9 (landscape), you need to convert to 9:16 (vertical) for Douyin/Shorts.

**Cropping strategy for chess board content**:
- Center the crop on the board. The board should fill most of the vertical frame.
- Leave room at the top and bottom for text overlays and captions.
- If you use a facecam in the full video, you can include a small facecam in the corner of the vertical version, or remove it entirely.
- If the board is shown full-screen in the original, crop to show the relevant section of the board rather than the entire board with empty space on the sides.

**Recommended crop layout (9:16)**:
```
┌──────────────┐
│  Text/Hook   │
│              │
│  ┌────────┐  │
│  │ Chess  │  │
│  │ Board  │  │
│  │(cropped│  │
│  │section)│  │
│  └────────┘  │
│              │
│  Caption/CTA │
└──────────────┘
```

**Step 3: Add Captions and Text Overlays**

- Add a hook text at the top of the screen for the first 3-5 seconds
- Add move-by-move captions or arrows showing piece movement
- Add the CTA text overlay in the last 3 seconds
- Use consistent fonts and colors across all your clips for brand recognition

**Step 4: Add Audio**

- If your full analysis has verbal commentary, use that audio
- If the clip needs a different energy, add background music (use royalty-free tracks)
- Add subtle sound effects for key moments: a "click" for each move, a dramatic sound for the sacrifice
- Keep audio balanced. Voice should be clearly audible over any music.

**Step 5: Review and Export**

- Watch the clip once at normal speed. Does it make sense without context? Does the hook grab you?
- Check that the clip is under 60 seconds
- Export at 1080x1920, 30fps, high bitrate
- File naming convention: `[date]-[game]-clip[number].mp4` (e.g., `20260320-huronghua-clip1.mp4`)

## 5. End-of-Clip CTAs That Drive Traffic

The CTA is the bridge between your short clip and your long-form content. Here are proven CTA formats ranked by effectiveness.

### Most Effective CTAs

| CTA Text | Where to Place | Why It Works |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| 完整解说在B站搜[你的ID] | End card, last 3 seconds | Direct instruction, easy to follow |
| "Full analysis on my YouTube channel - link in description" | Verbal + text overlay | Works for YouTube Shorts to YouTube long-form pipeline |
| 想知道后面怎么走的？主页有完整视频 | Text overlay at end | Creates curiosity gap |
| 这只是第一步妙手，后面还有更精彩的 | Verbal, cut mid-sequence | Cliffhanger approach, very effective |
| 关注我看完整对局解说 | Persistent small text at bottom | Low friction, always visible |

### The Cliffhanger Technique

The most powerful CTA is to cut the clip before the full resolution. Show the sacrifice, show 2-3 follow-up moves, then cut right before the final checkmate with text: "想看结果？完整视频在主页" (Want to see the result? Full video on my profile). This creates an irresistible curiosity gap that drives clicks to your profile and full video.

**Warning**: Do not overuse the cliffhanger. If every clip cuts before the payoff, viewers will get frustrated. Use it for 1 in 3 clips maximum. The other clips should deliver a complete, satisfying moment.

## 6. Ten Clip Template Ideas

Use these templates as starting points. Each one describes a specific type of clip you can cut from different game scenarios.

### Template 1: The Sacrifice Reveal
- Setup: Show a position that looks balanced
- Hook: "Red plays a move that looks insane"
- Action: Show the sacrifice, then 2-3 follow-up moves that justify it
- End: Eval bar showing the swing, CTA to full analysis

### Template 2: The Puzzle Challenge
- Setup: Freeze on a position
- Hook: "白先胜，你能找到吗？" (White to move and win, can you find it?)
- Action: Pause 5 seconds, then reveal the answer move by move
- End: "More puzzles on my profile"

### Template 3: The Historical Moment
- Setup: Brief text overlay with year and player names
- Hook: "This move won the 1982 national championship"
- Action: Show the decisive sequence from the famous game
- End: "Full game analysis on Bilibili/YouTube"

### Template 4: The Engine vs Human
- Setup: Show engine suggestion alongside the human move
- Hook: "The grandmaster played a move the engine rated as a mistake. But was it?"
- Action: Show why the human move was actually brilliant in context
- End: "Who do you trust — human or machine? Full debate in the long version"

### Template 5: The Beginner Trap
- Setup: Show a common opening position
- Hook: "If you play this natural-looking move, you lose immediately"
- Action: Show the trap, the refutation, and the correct move
- End: "Full opening guide on my channel"

### Template 6: The Comeback
- Setup: Show a position where one side is clearly losing (use eval bar)
- Hook: "Red was down a rook. Then this happened."
- Action: Show the turning point and 3-4 moves of the comeback
- End: Cliffhanger cut before the final result

### Template 7: The Speed Round
- Setup: Rapid-fire sequence of the best moves from a game
- Hook: "The best 30 seconds of chess you will see today"
- Action: Show 6-8 moves at fast pace with dramatic music
- End: "Slow-motion analysis on Bilibili"

### Template 8: The Mistake Breakdown
- Setup: Show the losing move
- Hook: "This one move cost black the entire game"
- Action: Show why the move was wrong and what should have been played instead
- End: "Learn from this mistake — full analysis on my channel"

### Template 9: The Pattern Recognition
- Setup: Show two different games side by side or in sequence
- Hook: "Same pattern, 40 years apart"
- Action: Show the recurring tactical pattern in both games
- End: "Understanding patterns is the key to improvement. More on my channel."

### Template 10: The Countdown
- Setup: "Top 3 moves of the week" or "Top 3 sacrifices from this tournament"
- Hook: "Number 3 is impressive. Number 1 is unbelievable."
- Action: Show #3, then #2, then #1 with increasing drama
- End: "Full analysis of each game on Bilibili/YouTube"

## Batch Production Workflow

For maximum efficiency, process multiple clips in one editing session.

1. **After recording a full analysis**: Immediately note 3-5 clip timestamps
2. **Weekly clip session**: Once per week, sit down and cut all clips from that week's full videos
3. **Export all clips**: Batch export in one go
4. **Schedule publishing**: Spread clips throughout the week. Do not publish 5 clips on the same day.
5. **Track which full videos the clips came from**: In your engagement spreadsheet, note which clip drove the most traffic back to the full video

**Target output**: For every 1 full game analysis (15-25 minutes), produce 2-3 highlight clips (30-60 seconds each). If you publish 2 full analyses per week, you should have 4-6 clips per week for Douyin and YouTube Shorts.
