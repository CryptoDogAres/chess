# Bilibili Title Optimization Guide — First 8 Characters

A practical guide to crafting Bilibili video titles where the first 8 characters do all the heavy lifting. Every template, rule, and example is tailored specifically to xiangqi (Chinese chess) content on B站.

---

## Why the First 8 Characters Matter on Bilibili

### Feed Truncation

Bilibili's mobile feed, recommendation cards, and search results truncate titles at different points depending on the device and display context:

- **Mobile feed (推荐页):** Titles display approximately **20-22 characters** before truncation, but the first line of a two-line title card shows only about **12-14 characters**.
- **Search results (搜索结果):** Approximately **18-20 characters** visible before the ellipsis.
- **PC sidebar recommendations (右侧推荐):** As few as **14-16 characters**.
- **Push notifications (消息推送):** Often only **10-12 characters**.

Across all these contexts, the first 8 characters are the **only guaranteed visible portion** of your title. If those 8 characters do not communicate value, curiosity, or relevance, the viewer scrolls past — regardless of how brilliant the rest of the title is.

### The Scanning Brain

Bilibili users scroll through dozens of videos per minute. Eye-tracking studies on content feeds show that users spend approximately 0.8-1.2 seconds per card before deciding to stop or scroll. In that window, the eye lands on the thumbnail first, then jumps to the **beginning** of the title. It rarely reaches the end.

This means your title is functionally two parts:
1. **Characters 1-8:** The hook. This is what gets read. This is where the decision happens.
2. **Characters 9+:** The context. This provides detail for viewers who are already interested.

Structure every title with this hierarchy in mind.

### Bilibili's Search and Recommendation Algorithm

Bilibili's recommendation system indexes the **full title** for keyword matching, but human behavior filters by the **visible portion**. This creates a dual optimization problem:

- The full title must contain relevant keywords (象棋, 残局, specific player names, opening names) for algorithmic discovery.
- The first 8 characters must contain an emotional or curiosity hook for human click-through.

The solution: front-load the hook, back-load the keywords.

## 20 Proven Title Templates for Xiangqi Content

### Category 1: 【标签】Opening Series (Bracket Tags)

Bracket tags at the start of a title instantly signal content category. They are Bilibili's native language — viewers are trained to recognize them.

1. **【象棋残局】** + description
   - Example: 【象棋残局】这步你绝对想不到
   - The tag takes 6 characters, so the next 2 characters must hook: "这步" (this move) immediately creates specificity.

2. **【象棋教学】** + topic
   - Example: 【象棋教学】三步杀的万能公式
   - Works because "三步杀" in the visible portion promises a concrete, learnable outcome.

3. **【实战解析】** + game detail
   - Example: 【实战解析】许银川这步棋太秀了
   - Name recognition + emotional reaction in the first 14 characters.

4. **【象棋挑战】** + challenge description
   - Example: 【象棋挑战】10秒内找到唯一解
   - Time pressure creates urgency even in a title.

### Category 2: Emotional Hooks (情感冲击)

Emotional openers grab attention through surprise, admiration, or disbelief. Use sparingly — if every video uses "震惊", the word loses power.

5. **震惊！** + surprising fact
   - Example: 震惊！业余棋手一步棋击败特级大师
   - "震惊！" is 3 characters including punctuation, leaving 5 characters to establish the surprise.

6. **太秀了！** + description
   - Example: 太秀了！这步弃车将杀绝了
   - "太秀了" is B站 native slang (meaning "incredible play"), resonates with the platform's culture.

7. **绝了！** + what's incredible
   - Example: 绝了！一步闷宫杀直接锁定胜局
   - Short emotional exclamation leaves more room for content description.

8. **离谱！** + outrageous situation
   - Example: 离谱！他居然用一个兵赢了整盘棋
   - "离谱" (absurd/outrageous) is high-energy B站 vernacular.

### Category 3: Numbers First (数字开头)

Numbers stand out visually in a sea of Chinese characters. They break the pattern and draw the eye.

9. **3步杀！** + position description
   - Example: 3步杀！红方先走你能看出来吗？
   - Small, odd numbers work best. "3" is more clickable than "7" because it feels achievable.

10. **10秒挑战** + puzzle description
    - Example: 10秒挑战：找到唯一的绝杀棋
    - Time-based numbers create urgency.

11. **99%的人** + mistake/failure
    - Example: 99%的人第一步就走错了这个残局
    - Percentage statistics trigger the "I'm in the 1%" ego response.

12. **第1步就错** + context
    - Example: 第1步就错！这个残局太有迷惑性了
    - Implies the viewer will also get it wrong, creating a challenge.

### Category 4: Name Recognition (名人开头)

Famous player names function as brand recognition. Fans search for these names, and even non-fans recognize authority.

13. **许银川教你** + skill
    - Example: 许银川教你如何破解当头炮开局
    - "教你" (teaches you) promises direct value from an authority.

14. **王天一的** + notable attribute
    - Example: 王天一的这步棋让解说都沉默了
    - Possessive structure + emotional reaction creates a story.

15. **赵鑫鑫vs** + opponent
    - Example: 赵鑫鑫vs王天一：最后一步神来之笔
    - "vs" is universally understood and signals competitive drama.

16. **胡荣华** + historical significance
    - Example: 胡荣华巅峰时期最强一战（完整复盘）
    - Historical legend + superlative claim + completeness promise.

### Category 5: Questions (问句开头)

Questions create an open loop in the reader's mind. The brain automatically begins searching for an answer, which makes clicking feel like a natural next step.

17. **这步你会走吗？** + context
    - Example: 这步你会走吗？大师和业余的分水岭
    - Direct address ("你") makes it personal.

18. **你能看出来吗** + puzzle
    - Example: 你能看出来吗？这里藏着一步绝杀
    - Challenge framed as a question feels less aggressive than a statement.

19. **为什么** + counterintuitive situation
    - Example: 为什么大师选择弃车？看完你就懂了
    - "为什么" promises an explanation for something confusing.

20. **怎么赢？** + difficult situation
    - Example: 怎么赢？只剩一车一马对满子
    - Presents a seemingly impossible problem, viewer clicks to see the solution.

## A/B Test Results Framework

Bilibili does not have a built-in A/B testing feature for titles, but you can test systematically using the following approach.

### Method: Sequential Testing

1. **Publish with Title A.** Record impressions (展现量), CTR (点击率), and views at the 24-hour and 72-hour marks.
2. **Change to Title B after 72 hours.** Record the same metrics for the next 72-hour window.
3. **Compare CTR** — this is the primary metric. Views can be affected by algorithmic momentum, but CTR isolates title performance.

### What to Test

| Test Variable | Version A | Version B | What You Learn |
|---|---|---|---|
| Emotional vs. Neutral | 绝了！这步弃车将杀 | 弃车将杀的精彩残局 | Does emotion boost CTR? |
| Number vs. No Number | 3步杀残局挑战 | 残局挑战：能找到绝杀吗 | Do numbers draw more clicks? |
| Name vs. No Name | 许银川的经典残局 | 这步残局太经典了 | Does name recognition matter? |
| Question vs. Statement | 这步你会走吗？ | 大多数人走错这步棋 | Which creates more curiosity? |
| Bracket Tag vs. No Tag | 【象棋残局】绝杀挑战 | 象棋残局绝杀挑战 | Do brackets boost or waste space? |
| Short vs. Long | 3步杀！你行吗？(9 chars) | 3步杀残局挑战：红方先走你能找到答案吗？(20 chars) | Ideal length for your audience |

### Tracking Template

For each video, record:

```
Video ID: ___
Title A: ___
Title A Period: [date] to [date]
Title A Impressions: ___
Title A CTR: ___
Title B: ___
Title B Period: [date] to [date]
Title B Impressions: ___
Title B CTR: ___
Winner: A / B
Hypothesis confirmed: Y / N
```

Run at least 10 tests before drawing conclusions about what works for your specific audience.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Keyword Stuffing at the Front

**Bad:** "象棋残局教学实战解析——许银川经典对局"
**Problem:** The first 8 characters ("象棋残局教学实战") are all keywords with zero emotional pull. This reads like a search query, not a title. Algorithms may index it well, but humans will not click.
**Fix:** "绝了！许银川这步残局棋太秀了（象棋教学）" — Hook first, keywords in parentheses at the end.

### Mistake 2: Vague Openings

**Bad:** "一盘很精彩的象棋"
**Problem:** "一盘很精彩" could describe literally anything. There is no specificity, no curiosity gap, no reason to click THIS video over any other.
**Fix:** "3步弃车绝杀！这盘棋精彩到离谱" — Specific detail (3 moves, sacrifice) creates a concrete mental image.

### Mistake 3: Titles That Are Too Long

**Bad:** "2023年全国象棋甲级联赛第七轮许银川执红对阵王天一屏风马对中炮完整复盘加解说"
**Problem:** 38 characters. By the time the viewer reads half of this, they have already scrolled past. Also, Bilibili will truncate this aggressively on mobile.
**Fix:** "许银川vs王天一：一步棋定胜负｜甲级联赛复盘" — 21 characters. All critical information in the first 12.

### Mistake 4: Clickbait Without Payoff

**Bad:** "你永远猜不到接下来发生了什么！！！"
**Problem:** Pure clickbait with no content signal. Even if it gets clicks, it will attract the wrong audience, get poor retention, and damage your channel's algorithmic profile. Bilibili's audience is particularly hostile to empty clickbait — expect negative 弹幕 and low 点赞/投币.
**Fix:** "你猜不到他下一步走了什么——弃双车绝杀" — The reveal is in the title itself, which actually increases clicks because the viewer wants to see HOW it happened.

### Mistake 5: English-Heavy Titles

**Bad:** "Chinese Chess Endgame Puzzle — Can You Solve It?"
**Problem:** Bilibili's primary audience reads Chinese. English titles get filtered out by most Chinese-speaking users' scanning patterns. Even bilingual viewers scan Chinese first on B站.
**Fix:** "象棋残局挑战：你能解出来吗？(Chess Puzzle)" — Chinese first, English in parentheses only if targeting bilingual audience.

### Mistake 6: No Punctuation or Rhythm

**Bad:** "象棋残局红方三步杀挑战试试看"
**Problem:** A wall of characters with no visual breaks. The eye has no anchor point. Punctuation, brackets, and symbols create visual rhythm that aids scanning.
**Fix:** "3步杀！红方先走——你能找到吗？" — Exclamation mark, dash, and question mark create three distinct visual beats.

## Character Count Guide

### Ideal Title Length: 15-25 Characters

| Length | Assessment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Under 10 | Too short | Lacks context; may not contain enough keywords for search |
| 10-14 | Acceptable for puzzles | Works for simple, punchy content: "3步杀！你行吗？" |
| **15-20** | **Optimal** | **Best balance of hook + context + keywords** |
| **20-25** | **Good** | **Allows for more detail; still displays well on most surfaces** |
| 25-30 | Getting long | Will truncate on mobile; make sure characters 1-8 carry the hook |
| Over 30 | Too long | Almost certainly truncated everywhere; cut it down |

### Character Counting Tips

- Chinese characters, English letters, and numbers each count as 1 character in Bilibili's system.
- Punctuation counts: ！、？、【】、—— all take character slots.
- Bracket tags like 【象棋残局】 consume 6 characters. Use them only when the category signal is worth the character cost.
- The vertical bar ｜ (full-width) is a popular Bilibili title separator. It costs 1 character but adds valuable visual structure.

### Recommended Structure

```
[Hook: 6-10 characters] + [Detail: 5-10 characters] + [Keyword/Tag: 3-8 characters]

Examples:
绝了！这步弃车(7) + 直接锁定胜局(6) + ｜象棋残局(5) = 18 characters
3步杀挑战(5) + ：红方先走你能找到吗(10) = 15 characters
许银川这步棋(6) + 让解说都沉默了(7) + ｜实战复盘(5) = 18 characters
```

## Final Checklist for Every Title

Before publishing, run your title through these checks:

1. **Read only the first 8 characters.** Do they alone make you want to click? If not, rewrite.
2. **Does the title contain at least one keyword** (象棋, 残局, a player name, an opening name) for search discoverability?
3. **Is the title between 15 and 25 characters?** If not, adjust.
4. **Does the title have visual rhythm?** At least one punctuation mark or bracket to break up the character wall.
5. **Is the promise specific?** "精彩" (wonderful) is vague. "弃双车三步杀" (sacrifice two rooks, checkmate in three) is specific.
6. **Does the title match the thumbnail?** The title and thumbnail must tell the same story. Contradictions confuse the viewer and reduce CTR.
7. **Would you click this title** if you saw it while casually scrolling B站? Be honest.
