# Douyin to YouTube Shorts Repurposing Guide

A step-by-step workflow for adapting your best Douyin content to YouTube Shorts, maximizing reach without doubling your production effort.

---

## Why Repurpose Douyin Content to YouTube Shorts

You are already creating short-form vertical video for Douyin. YouTube Shorts uses the same format, the same aspect ratio, and the same audience behavior (swipe, watch, engage). By repurposing your Douyin content, you can reach an international audience with minimal extra work. The xiangqi community on YouTube is smaller but growing, and English-language xiangqi content has very little competition.

The key principle: **create once, adapt twice**. Your Douyin video is the source material. Your YouTube Short is an adapted version, not a direct re-upload.

---

## 1. What to Repurpose

Not every Douyin video is worth repurposing. Focus on content that translates well across language and culture barriers.

### Best Candidates for Repurposing

| Content Type | Why It Works on YouTube | Priority |
|-------------|------------------------|----------|
| Puzzle videos (残局谜题) | Visual, minimal language needed, universal appeal | Highest |
| Highlight clips (精彩片段) | Dramatic moments transcend language | High |
| Sacrifice/dramatic moves | Shock value works in any language | High |
| Quick tips (小技巧) | Practical value, easy to subtitle | Medium |
| Interactive "What would you play?" | Engagement format works globally | Medium |

### Skip These for Repurposing

- Long commentary that depends heavily on spoken Chinese nuance
- Content referencing Douyin-specific trends or memes that a global audience will not understand
- Videos with Chinese text overlays that are too complex to translate
- Content that performed poorly on Douyin (if it did not work there, it probably will not work on YouTube either)

**Rule of thumb**: If a video got above-average engagement on Douyin, it is a repurposing candidate. If it was in your top 25% for the month, definitely repurpose it.

## 2. Technical Specifications

Both platforms use the same core video specs, which makes repurposing straightforward.

| Spec | Douyin | YouTube Shorts |
|------|--------|---------------|
| Aspect Ratio | 9:16 (vertical) | 9:16 (vertical) |
| Resolution | 1080 x 1920 | 1080 x 1920 |
| Max Length | 10 minutes (but short = under 60s) | Under 60 seconds |
| File Format | MP4 | MP4 |
| Frame Rate | 30fps or 60fps | 30fps or 60fps |
| Audio | AAC | AAC |

**Important**: If your Douyin video is between 60-90 seconds, you will need to trim it to under 60 seconds for YouTube Shorts. Identify the strongest 50-55 seconds and cut the rest. Often the intro or outro can be shortened.

## 3. What to Change: The Adaptation Checklist

This is the core of the repurposing workflow. Do not just re-upload your Douyin video directly to YouTube. Make these changes.

### 3.1 Remove the Douyin Watermark

Douyin automatically adds a watermark to downloaded videos. YouTube will suppress videos with visible watermarks from other platforms.

**How to avoid the watermark**:
- Do NOT download the video from Douyin and re-upload it.
- Instead, go back to 剪映 (CapCut Chinese version) and export the original project file without the Douyin watermark.
- If you no longer have the project file, use the "save to local" option in Douyin before publishing, which saves a clean version to your camera roll.
- As a last resort, crop slightly to remove the watermark, but this reduces video quality.

### 3.2 Add English Subtitles or Captions

This is the single most important adaptation step. Your Douyin video likely has Chinese narration or text overlays. For YouTube, you need English.

**Option A: Hardcoded English subtitles**
- Open the project in CapCut (English version of 剪映)
- Use CapCut's auto-caption feature, then manually translate the captions to English
- Position subtitles in the lower third, white text with black outline, font size 28-32
- Keep translations concise. You do not need word-for-word translation. Convey the key message.

**Option B: Bilingual subtitles**
- Show Chinese text on top, English translation below
- This appeals to both Chinese-speaking diaspora and English-speaking learners
- Use slightly smaller font (24-26) to fit both lines

**Option C: Minimal text overlay**
- For puzzle videos, you may not need full subtitles
- Add simple English text: "White to move and win" or "Can you find the checkmate?"
- Use arrows or highlight circles on the board instead of verbal explanation

### 3.3 Adjust the Title for YouTube SEO

Douyin titles are optimized for Chinese-speaking users and the Douyin algorithm. YouTube requires different keyword strategy.

**Douyin title style**: Short, punchy, uses Chinese internet slang or trending phrases.
**YouTube Shorts title style**: Descriptive, keyword-rich, English (or bilingual).

### 3.4 Create a Thumbnail

On Douyin, there is no separate thumbnail. The platform auto-selects a frame from the video, and most viewers encounter your video through the swipe feed, not a thumbnail.

On YouTube, thumbnails matter even for Shorts. YouTube shows Shorts thumbnails on your channel page, in search results, and in the Shorts shelf on desktop.

**Thumbnail guidelines for YouTube Shorts**:
- Use a clear, high-contrast board position showing the critical moment
- Add minimal text overlay (1-3 words max): "Checkmate!" or "Find the Win"
- Use bright colors and high contrast. Red and yellow perform well.
- Show the board from a consistent angle across all your Shorts for brand recognition
- Size: 1080 x 1920 (same as the video)

### 3.5 Adapt Hashtags

Douyin and YouTube use completely different hashtag ecosystems.

**Douyin hashtags (for reference)**:
#象棋 #中国象棋 #残局 #象棋教学 #下棋 #棋牌

**YouTube Shorts hashtags**:
#xiangqi #chinesechess #chess #checkmate #chesspuzzle #shorts #chessstrategy #boardgames #chinesechessstrategy #learnchess

Always include #Shorts in your YouTube hashtags. Use 5-8 hashtags per video. Place them in the description, not the title.

## 4. Posting Schedule

Timing matters for both platforms, and you want to give Douyin the first-mover advantage since it is your primary platform with the larger audience.

### The 24-48 Hour Rule

1. **Day 0**: Publish on Douyin. This is your primary launch.
2. **Day 1-2**: After 24-48 hours, adapt and upload to YouTube Shorts.

**Why the delay?**
- Douyin's algorithm gives the strongest push in the first 24 hours. You want full algorithmic attention on Douyin first.
- If both platforms see identical content posted simultaneously, neither benefits from exclusivity.
- The delay gives you time to see Douyin performance data. If a video flopped on Douyin, you might skip the YouTube version or adjust it.

### Optimal Posting Times for YouTube Shorts

| Target Audience | Best Time (UTC) | Best Days |
|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| US/English speakers | 14:00-18:00 UTC (morning US time) | Tuesday-Thursday |
| Chinese diaspora | 00:00-04:00 UTC (evening US/Asia overlap) | Weekdays |
| Global mix | 13:00-16:00 UTC | Wednesday-Friday |

Start with the "Global mix" timing and adjust based on your YouTube Studio analytics after 2-3 weeks.

## 5. Tools and Workflow

Here is the exact tool chain for repurposing one video.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

1. **In 剪映 (CapCut CN)**: Open the original Douyin project
2. **Export**: Export without watermark as MP4, 1080x1920, 30fps
3. **Open in CapCut (English version)**: Import the clean MP4
4. **Add English captions**: Use auto-caption, then translate and edit
5. **Adjust text overlays**: Translate any Chinese text overlays to English
6. **Trim if needed**: Cut to under 60 seconds
7. **Export from CapCut**: MP4, 1080x1920, high quality
8. **Create thumbnail**: Screenshot the key moment, add text in Canva or CapCut
9. **Upload to YouTube**: Add English title, description, hashtags, thumbnail
10. **Schedule or publish**: Set to publish at optimal time

### Tool Summary

| Step | Tool | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| Source export | 剪映 (CapCut CN) | Get clean video without Douyin watermark |
| Subtitles | CapCut (English) | Auto-caption + translation |
| Thumbnails | Canva or CapCut | Create YouTube thumbnail |
| Upload | YouTube Studio (mobile or desktop) | Publish the Short |
| Analytics | YouTube Studio | Track performance |

**Time estimate**: After the first few videos, the full repurposing workflow should take 15-25 minutes per video.

## 6. Title Translation Examples

Here are 10 real-world examples showing how to adapt Douyin Chinese titles to YouTube English titles. The goal is not literal translation but adaptation for a different audience and algorithm.

| # | Douyin Title (Chinese) | YouTube Shorts Title (English) | Notes |
|---|----------------------|-------------------------------|-------|
| 1 | 这步弃车你敢走吗？ | Would You Sacrifice Your Rook? | Question format drives engagement |
| 2 | 3步杀棋，你能找到吗 | Checkmate in 3 Moves - Can You Solve It? | Added "solve" for puzzle keyword |
| 3 | 残局挑战：红先胜 | Endgame Puzzle: Red to Move and Win | Clear, descriptive, SEO-friendly |
| 4 | 胡荣华的神之一手 | Hu Ronghua's Genius Move | Name recognition for xiangqi fans |
| 5 | 新手最常犯的错误 | The #1 Mistake Beginners Make in Xiangqi | "Number one" creates specificity and curiosity |
| 6 | 当头炮开局全解析 | Central Cannon Opening Explained in 60 Seconds | Added time constraint for Shorts appeal |
| 7 | AI算了10亿步也没找到这手棋 | The Move Even AI Couldn't Find | Shorter, more dramatic |
| 8 | 这步你会走吗？评论区见 | What Would You Play Here? | Removed Chinese-specific CTA, kept the question |
| 9 | 象棋史上最经典的弃马局 | The Most Famous Knight Sacrifice in Xiangqi History | Full description for YouTube search |
| 10 | 5秒内找到杀棋算你赢 | Find the Checkmate in 5 Seconds | Time challenge creates urgency |

### Title Adaptation Principles

1. **Do not translate literally**. Adapt for the audience. Chinese internet language is different from English YouTube language.
2. **Include the word "xiangqi" or "Chinese chess"** in at least half your titles. This is how new viewers discover you through search.
3. **Use proven YouTube title patterns**: "Can You Solve...?", "The #1...", "...in X Seconds", "What Would You...?"
4. **Keep titles under 60 characters** so they display fully on mobile.
5. **Front-load the hook**. Put the most interesting word or concept first, not last.

## Repurposing Checklist

Use this checklist for each video you repurpose. Print it out or keep it as a note on your phone.

- [ ] Original Douyin video selected (top 25% performer)
- [ ] Clean export from 剪映 without watermark
- [ ] Video is under 60 seconds
- [ ] English subtitles or captions added
- [ ] Chinese text overlays translated or replaced
- [ ] English title written (under 60 characters, includes "xiangqi" or "Chinese chess")
- [ ] Thumbnail created (clear board position, text overlay, high contrast)
- [ ] Description written with 5-8 English hashtags including #Shorts
- [ ] Scheduled 24-48 hours after Douyin publish date
- [ ] Performance tracked in engagement spreadsheet after 24h and 7d

## Scaling the Workflow

Once you are comfortable with the repurposing process, consider these efficiency improvements:

1. **Batch processing**: Repurpose 3-5 Douyin videos in one sitting instead of one at a time. Export all clean versions from 剪映 first, then do all subtitles in CapCut, then all uploads.

2. **Template everything**: Create a YouTube description template with your standard hashtags, channel links, and CTA. Paste and customize for each video.

3. **Hire a subtitle translator**: If your output increases, this is the first task to delegate. Provide the Chinese video and your preferred English title. They handle subtitles and upload.

4. **Track what performs differently**: Some content types may perform better on YouTube than on Douyin, or vice versa. Keep separate performance notes for each platform. Over time, you may create platform-specific content rather than only repurposing.

5. **Cross-promote**: In your Douyin bio or pinned comment, mention your YouTube channel. In your YouTube Shorts, add a subtle CTA for your Douyin. Build both audiences simultaneously.
